PROPERTY DESCRIPTION

TRACT ‘A-1’
A certain tract of land in Colonias, Taos County, New Mexico, within the Antonio
Martinez Grant; located within Projected Sections 25 & 36, T 26 N, R 12 E, NMPM;
described as being part of Tract 8, Map 14, Survey 2 of the 1941 Taos County
Reassessment Survey; and more particularly described by metes and bounds as follows:
BEGINNING at the West corner of this tract, from whence Triangulation Station
‘Velvet’, a 1973 State Engineer’s Office brass cap monument, bears S 14° 11’ W, 5300.0
feet. distant, and from whence a 1/2 in. rebar found as a witness comer, bears, S 42° 00° E,
6.0 feet. distant, thence;

N 23° 40’ E, 529.1 feet. to the North corner, from whence a 1/2 in. rebar

found as a witness corner, bears S 43° 26° E, 10.0 feet. distant, thence;

S 43° 26° E, 366.6 feet. to a 1/2 in. rebar found, thence;

S 26° 08° W, 529.4 feet. to a 1/2 in. rebar set, thence;

N 42° 00’ W, 345.6 feet. to the POINT AND PLACE OF BEGINNING.
This tract contains 4.000 acres, more or less, and is subject to an easement for ingress,
egress and utilities along the NE boundary, all as shown on a LOT LINE ADJUSTMENT
& BOUNDARY SURVEY PLAT entitled “Lopez & Barrone”, survey no. 858-A, by
Jeffrey B. Stadler, NMLS no. 8327, dated December 18, 2003.
